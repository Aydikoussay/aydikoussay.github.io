06. Timeline

Red Team & Offensive Security Progression

Operator-oriented growth path from fundamentals to advanced adversary simulation.

Stage 1

Network & Systems Intelligence

TCP/IP, routing, packet analysis, service enumeration, traffic baselining, attack surface mapping.

Focus outcome: Understand how data moves and where visibility exists (IDS/EDR/NDR points).

Stage 2

Linux & Windows Internals + Exploitation Fundamentals

Linux CLI, Windows internals, processes, services, permissions, logging behavior.

Focus outcome: Understand what execution looks like from the OS perspective, not just "how to run commands".

Stage 3

Web & Identity Attack Surface

OWASP Top 10 + authentication systems + session handling + access control logic.

Focus outcome: Understand how identity flows break (auth tokens, sessions, role checks).

Stage 4

Privilege Escalation & Post-Exploitation

Linux + Windows privilege escalation concepts, credential access, and system pivoting (in labs).

Focus outcome: Understand how attackers transition from initial access → control → expansion.

Stage 5

Active Directory & Enterprise Attack Paths

AD enumeration, Kerberos concepts, NTLM relay concepts, delegation abuse, lateral movement theory.

Focus outcome: Understand enterprise identity trust abuse chains.

Stage 6

Command & Control (C2) Operations

C2 architecture concepts (beaconing vs interactive, HTTP/S, DNS, SMB transports), operator workflow (tasking, in-memory execution, session management), infrastructure design (staging vs command, OpSec separation), and detection reality — every C2 action produces telemetry.

Focus outcome: Measuring and controlling detection exposure in authorized environments.

Stage 7

Adversary Simulation & Tradecraft Mapping

Mapping actions to MITRE ATT&CK patterns, simulating intrusion chains end-to-end, writing realistic attack scenarios for blue teams, understanding how SOCs detect behavior (not tools).

Focus outcome: Think like a threat actor + detection engineer simultaneously.

Stage 8

Advanced Operations (Red Team Engineering)

Detection awareness (what triggers EDR alerts, what network patterns look suspicious), operational security (minimizing footprint, reducing noise, segregating tooling), and campaign design (multi-stage attack planning, objective-driven engagement, adversary-style reporting).

Focus outcome: Operate like real adversaries are measured.
